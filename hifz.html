Program Overview

What is Hifz ul Quran?

Hifz — the complete memorization of the Holy Quran — is one of the highest honors a Muslim can achieve. A Hafiz carries the words of Allah in their heart, becoming a living guardian of the divine text.

At AlHassan Huffaz Academy, our Hifz program is built on three pillars: Tajweed (correct recitation), Tathbeet (firm retention), and Tarbiyah (character development). Students don't just memorize — they understand, internalize, and embody the Quran.

Tajweed First Every student masters correct pronunciation before memorization begins
Structured Daily Routine Sabaq, Sabqi and Manzil system ensures strong, lasting memorization
Individual Attention Small groups with dedicated Qari for each student's pace and progress
Curriculum Structure
نصاب کا ڈھانچہ

Three Phases to Complete Hifz

A carefully structured journey from foundation to completion — each phase builds on the last

01
Phase 1

Nazra + Tajweed

ناظرہ + تجوید

Foundation phase where students develop fluent Quran reading with proper Tajweed rules. No memorization until this is perfected.

  • Makharij (pronunciation points)
  • Sifaat of letters
  • Rules of Madd, Waqf & Ibtida
  • Daily fluency drills
3–6 Months
02
Phase 2

Active Hifz

حفظ فعال

The core memorization phase. Each day students learn new sabaq, revise recent sabqi, and recite older manzil portions.

  • New lesson (Sabaq) daily
  • Recent revision (Sabqi)
  • Older revision (Manzil)
  • Weekly Qari assessment
2–3 Years
03
Phase 3

Dour & Revision

دور و مراجعت

Consolidation and completion. The entire Quran is reviewed, strengthened, and tested before the student earns their Sanad.

  • Full Quran dour (3–4 cycles)
  • One-to-one Qari sessions
  • Public recitation practice
  • Sanad ceremony preparation
6–12 Months

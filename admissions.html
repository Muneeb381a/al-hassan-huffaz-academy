What You Need to Apply

Hifz ul Quran Program

حفظ القرآن داخلہ
Age: 5 to 13 years (boys & girls)
Quran Reading: Basic Nazra (qaida completed) preferred but not mandatory
Batches: Separate sections for boys and girls — both offered
Timings: Morning batch: 7:30 AM – 1:00 PM
Documents: B-Form copy, parent CNIC, 2 passport photos
Entry Test: Short Quran recitation and basic listening assessment
Note: Students who have not completed Nazra are still welcome — we provide a preparatory Qaida track before Hifz begins.

Academic Classes 6–9

تعلیمی کلاسز داخلہ
Age: 10–16 years (Class 6 to Class 9)
Previous Class: Must have passed the preceding class (report card required)
Board: FBISE affiliated — Class 9 board-registered students only
Timings: Morning: 8:00 AM – 2:00 PM (Mon–Sat)
Documents: Previous result card, B-Form, parent CNIC, 2 passport photos
Entry Test: Maths and English written test (30 minutes)
Note: Students combining Hifz + Academic classes are given a specially structured timetable to accommodate both programs without conflict.

Common Questions from Parents

Yes — we offer a Combined Program where students attend Hifz in the morning and Academic classes in the afternoon. A structured timetable is provided. Many of our successful students completed Class 9 and their Hifz simultaneously.
Yes. AlHassan Academy has fully separate facilities for girls — with dedicated female Qarias and teachers. The girls section maintains strict purdah and female-only environment at all times.
Yes — we accept students without prior Nazra completion. They are placed in our Qaida/Nazra preparatory track first. Once Nazra is complete (usually 3–6 months), they move directly into the Hifz program without re-enrollment.
Fees are paid monthly or quarterly — your choice. Payment is accepted in cash at the academy office between 8 AM – 2 PM on any school day. A receipt is provided for every payment. Fee for the upcoming month is due by the 5th of each month.
Yes. We believe no deserving child should be denied Islamic education due to financial hardship. Partial scholarships and fee waivers are available for eligible families. Simply mention this when filling the application form or speak directly with our principal.
The average student completes Hifz in 3–4 years, depending on the child's pace, consistency, and the amount of time dedicated daily. Some exceptional students complete in 2.5 years. We do not rush students — quality of memorization and Tajweed is our priority over speed.
